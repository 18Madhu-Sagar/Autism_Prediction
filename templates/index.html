Autism Prediction In Toddlers













1. Does the child make eye contact with others?



2. Does the child respond to their name when called?



3. Does the child point or use gestures to communicate?



4. Does the child have difficulty engaging in reciprocal social interactions with others?



5. Does the child show an interest in playing with toys or objects in an unusual manner?



6. Does the child have difficulty understanding or using language appropriately for their age?



7. Does the child have difficulty following social cues or understanding social rules?



8. Does the child engage in repetitive behaviors or have restricted interests?



9. Does the child have difficulty adapting to changes in routine or transitioning between activities?



10. Does the child exhibit any unusual sensory behaviors, such as avoiding certain textures or sounds?



11. Does the child respond appropriately to social cues (e.g., smiles, gestures)?



12. Does the child engage in babbling or vocalizations during social interactions?



13. Does the child use gestures to communicate (e.g., waving, pointing)?



14. Does the child show interest in interacting with peers?



15. Does the child follow another person's gaze or point to objects of interest?



16. Does the child understand and respond to others' emotions?



17. Does the child use facial expressions to communicate?



18. Does the child understand and follow simple instructions?



19. Does the child become upset by changes in routine or environment?



20. Does the child become strongly attached to specific objects?



21. Is the child sensitive to certain textures or materials?



22. Does the child show a fascination with spinning objects?



23. Does the child have an atypical response to pain (e.g., seeming not to notice injuries)?



24. Is the child sensitive to certain smells or odors?



25. Is the child sensitive to certain types of lighting or brightness?
